Anthroposophical eldercare honors the full biography of the human being. These communities, homes, and services provide care rooted in Steiner's understanding of aging as a spiritual passage, integrating artistic therapies, biography work, and meaningful social engagement alongside medical support.

"Aging with Grace: An Anthroposophical Approach to the Later Years"

In Memoriam  ·  Honoring those who carried the light

"The human being is not what he is, but what he is becoming."
- Rudolf Steiner

This space honors members of the anthroposophical community who have crossed the threshold. Search for someone below or browse the memorials. To add a tribute, use the form at the bottom.

Showing 6 memorials

Rudolf Steiner

1861 - 1925
Kraljevec, Austria-Hungary / Dornach, Switzerland

Founder of anthroposophy, Waldorf education, biodynamic agriculture, and anthroposophic medicine. His legacy continues through thousands of initiatives worldwide.

Ita Wegman

1876 - 1943
Karawang, Dutch East Indies / Arlesheim, Switzerland

Co-founder of anthroposophic medicine alongside Rudolf Steiner. Established the first anthroposophic clinic in Arlesheim, Switzerland in 1921.

EP

Ehrenfried Pfeiffer

1899 - 1961
Munich, Germany / Spring Valley, NY, USA

Pioneer of biodynamic agriculture in North America. Founded the Threefold Farm and developed soil testing methods used worldwide.

KK

Karl König

1902 - 1966
Vienna, Austria / Brachenreuthe, Germany

Founder of the Camphill movement in 1940, creating inclusive communities for people with developmental disabilities across the world.

Marie Steiner-von Sivers

1867 - 1948
Wloclawek, Poland / Beatenberg, Switzerland

Co-worker of Rudolf Steiner and guardian of his literary estate. Developed speech formation and dramatic arts as a path of spiritual practice.

AS

Albert Steffen

1884 - 1963
Wynau, Switzerland / Dornach, Switzerland

Swiss poet, playwright, and president of the General Anthroposophical Society from 1925 to 1963. Carried forward Steiner's artistic impulse.
